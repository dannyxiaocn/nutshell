## Social Media Commentary Framework

This skill teaches you how to consistently produce comments that stand out, add value, and build influence across social media platforms.

---

## Step 1 — Discover & Scout (热点发现)

### Finding Topics Worth Commenting On

```
1. SEARCH   → Use web_search to find trending topics in your domains
2. FILTER   → Skip noise: celebrity gossip, rage-bait, engagement farming
3. QUALIFY  → A topic is worth your time if:
              - It affects many people (scale)
              - The mainstream take is wrong or incomplete (opportunity)
              - You have a genuine edge or perspective (authenticity)
              - It's timely — the conversation window is still open (relevance)
4. DEEP-READ → Use fetch_url to read primary sources, not just headlines
```

### Source Priority

| Source Type | Trust Level | Usage |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Primary sources (papers, filings, official statements) | High | Base your analysis on these |
| Quality journalism (investigative, long-form) | Medium-High | Good for context and narrative |
| Social media threads | Medium | Good for sentiment, bad for facts |
| Hot takes and opinion pieces | Low | Good to know the mainstream angle, not to parrot |
| Headlines only | Very Low | Never comment based on headlines alone |

## Step 2 — Analyze & Extract Angles (观点提炼)

### The Angle-Finding Framework

For any trending topic, systematically check these lenses:

#### Lens 1: Incentive Analysis (利益分析)
- Who benefits from this outcome?
- Who is pushing this narrative, and why?
- Follow the money — what business model underlies this?

#### Lens 2: Second-Order Effects (连锁反应)
- If this trend continues, what happens in 6 months? 2 years?
- What unintended consequences will emerge?
- Who will be affected that nobody is talking about yet?

#### Lens 3: Historical Pattern Matching (历史对照)
- Has something similar happened before?
- What was the outcome last time?
- What's different this time that might change the result?

#### Lens 4: Contrarian Check (逆向思维)
- What if the mainstream take is wrong?
- What evidence would disprove the consensus?
- Is this a bubble, a hype cycle, or a real shift?

#### Lens 5: Cross-Domain Connection (跨界关联)
- How does this tech trend connect to cultural shifts?
- How does this policy change affect business models?
- What can other industries learn from this?

## Step 3 — Craft the Comment (评论撰写)

### Comment Anatomy

Every strong comment follows this structure:

```
HOOK    → 1 sentence that stops the scroll (insight, surprising fact, bold claim)
BODY    → 2-3 sentences of supporting reasoning, evidence, or analogy
CLOSER  → 1 sentence: thought-provoker, question, or actionable takeaway
```

### Platform-Specific Tone Guide

#### Twitter / X
- **Length**: 1-3 tweets max. Thread only for complex topics.
- **Tone**: Punchy, witty, direct. Lead with your strongest line.
- **Format**: Short sentences. Use line breaks. Numbers and data stand out.
- **Example**:
  > Everyone is celebrating [X]. Nobody is asking who pays for it.
  > 
  > Three things to watch: [1] [2] [3]
  > 
  > The real story isn't the announcement — it's what was left out.

#### Reddit
- **Length**: 1-2 paragraphs typical. Longer for high-effort subs.
- **Tone**: Analytical, evidence-based, slightly informal. Show your work.
- **Format**: Use quotes to reference the post. Cite sources. Structure with paragraphs.
- **Example**:
  > The top comments here are all focused on [surface issue], but the real question is [deeper issue].
  > 
  > I worked in [relevant field] for [N years], and what this actually means is [insight]. Here's why: [evidence]. The last time something similar happened was [precedent], and the result was [outcome].

#### LinkedIn
- **Length**: 3-5 short paragraphs. Use line breaks aggressively.
- **Tone**: Professional but authentic. No buzzword soup. Be the person who says what everyone thinks but nobody posts.
- **Format**: Personal anecdote → industry insight → actionable takeaway.

#### 微博 / 小红书 / 知乎
- **Length**: Varies by platform. 知乎 rewards depth; 微博 rewards brevity.
- **Tone**: Can be more personal and colloquial. Humor works well.
- **Format**: Use platform-native elements (知乎 structured answers, 小红书 emoji-rich).

### Quality Checklist

Before posting, verify:
- [ ] Is my core insight original or at least uncommonly stated?
- [ ] Did I back my claim with evidence, logic, or a concrete example?
- [ ] Could this be shorter without losing substance?
- [ ] Am I attacking ideas, not people?
- [ ] Would I stand behind this comment if it went viral?

## Step 4 — Engage & Follow Up (跟进互动)

### Engagement Strategy

After posting, the work isn't done:

```
1. MONITOR  → Check for replies within 1-2 hours of posting
2. RESPOND  → Engage substantively with thoughtful replies
3. AMPLIFY  → If a thread develops, add follow-up insights
4. LEARN    → Note which angles resonated and which fell flat
```

### Response Playbook

| Reply Type | Your Response |
|------------|---------------|
| **Thoughtful disagreement** | Acknowledge their point, then clarify or expand yours with additional evidence |
| **Adds new information** | Thank them, integrate the new data into your analysis, update your position if warranted |
| **Hostile / bad faith** | One calm, factual response max. Then disengage. Never wrestle with pigs. |
| **Asks for more detail** | Happy to elaborate — this is your chance to go deeper |
| **Agrees enthusiastically** | Brief thanks, then add a new related insight to keep the thread valuable |

### Building Threads

When a topic deserves extended analysis:

1. **Opening tweet/post** — the hook and core insight
2. **Evidence post** — data, charts, examples supporting the claim
3. **Counter-argument** — the strongest objection to your own point, and why you still hold your position
4. **Implications** — what this means for the reader, practically
5. **Call to discussion** — invite specific perspectives or questions

## Meta-Strategy: Building a Commentary Voice

### Consistency Principles

1. **Pick your lanes** — Focus on 3-5 domains where you have genuine depth
2. **Develop signature frameworks** — reusable analytical lenses that become your brand
3. **Be predictably honest** — your audience should know you'll call BS regardless of which "side" it's on
4. **Show receipts** — reference your previous predictions and analyses, especially when you were right
5. **Evolve publicly** — when you change your mind, explain why. This builds more credibility than always being "right"

### Timing

- **Breaking news**: Comment within 1-2 hours for maximum visibility
- **Trending discussions**: Add your voice within the first 24 hours
- **Evergreen analysis**: Can be posted anytime, but tie to a current hook for relevance
- **Contrarian takes**: Wait 24-48 hours until the mainstream consensus crystallizes, then challenge it

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Instead |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Commenting on everything | Dilutes your voice, looks desperate | Be selective; quality over quantity |
| Pure negativity | People tune out constant critics | Criticize constructively; propose alternatives |
| Humblebragging | Transparent and annoying | If you have relevant experience, state it directly |
| Thread-jacking | Derails conversation, annoys OP | Start your own post if the topic is different |
| Echo-chambering | Adds zero value | If you agree, add a NEW insight, don't just "+1" |
| Over-qualifying | "I might be wrong but maybe possibly..." kills your credibility | State your position clearly, acknowledge uncertainty specifically |
